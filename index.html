ETF BASICS
How ETFs Work in India
📊
What is an ETF?
A basket of securities trading on NSE/BSE like a stock. Tracks an index and gives instant diversification at very low cost.
🏛️
NSE vs BSE
NSE ETFs have higher liquidity. BSE lists some unique ETFs. Both regulated by SEBI. You can buy on either through any broker.
💰
Expense Ratio
Annual fee charged by the fund house. Indian ETFs range from 0.04% (Nifty 50) to 0.65% (Gold ETFs). Lower is better.
📈
How to Buy
Open a demat account with Zerodha, Groww, or Upstox. Search the ticker and buy during market hours 9:15am–3:30pm IST.
🔄
Rebalancing
Index reviewed semi-annually or quarterly. Companies that fall below criteria are replaced. Your ETF auto-rebalances — no action needed.
🛡️
Safety
SEBI-regulated. Units held safely in your demat. Underlying assets held by a custodian separately from the AMC. Fully protected.
